National Society of Black Engineers NSBE Jr. Chapter

NSBE Jr. Chapters are designed to stimulate the interest in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics fields, or STEM. The goal is to encourage students to attend college and pursue technical degrees. We meet on a weekly basis and provide activities to help students discover the excitement of academic excellence, leadership, technical development and teamwork. The ACCESS NSBE Jr. Chapter is open to 9th-12th grade students regardless of race or gender. Weekly meetings are held in Bridgeport every Thursday from 6pm to 8pm and in New Haven every Monday from 6pm to 8pm. This programs runs from September to May.

Saturday STEAM Club

The Science, Technology, Engineering, Art and Mathematics club is a program aimed at both boys and girls in grades K through 8th and is designed to provide a jumpstart on STEM early career awareness through many fun and hands on project based activities and competitions. The program meets one Saturday per month and runs from September to June. The program is currently filled for the 2015-2016 year.

Summer STEAM Enrichment

This summer two-week enrichment program will assist with summer learning loss through fun, engaging and hands activities that include will include Fun Math and Vocabulary Games, Robotics, Nanotechnology, 3D Printing, and some Amazing Art & Engineering Projects and is open to all students entering 4th-8th grades. Program will run Monday, August 8th to Friday, August 19th, 2016 from 9am to 4pm.

"She Looks Just Like Me"- All Girl's STEM Event

The free one-day community event is for minority girls between the ages of 11 to 17, who reside in Bridgeport and the surrounding cities, to come together to explore, enhance their knowledge and increase their interest in careers in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics as well as learn how to foster positive self-images and be successful in school. Save the Date for our 3rd Annual SLJLM, Saturday, November, 5, 2016.
